Arancini

Description

Arancini translates from Italian as "little orange", because these balls are similar to it in shape and color. If you suddenly have a large supply of boiled rice in the refrigerator, and guests are already knocking on the door, this recipe will become a lifesaver. The original version involves stuffing with minced meat, green peas, celery, carrots and onions, but you can cook a simple but delicious variation of the Italian dish.

Ingridients

  • boiled round (or any other with a viscous consistency) rice or pre-cooked risotto
  • with 1 small ball of mozzarella for each arancini
  • flour, eggs and breadcrumbs
  • deep-frying oil

Steps

  1. Take some rice and flatten it on the palm of your hand â€” the layer should be such that the filling does not leak out during cooking.
  2. Place a ball of mozzarella in the center, cover with rice on all sides and form a ball.
  3. Roll it in flour, then in beaten egg and breadcrumbs.
  4. Fry in preheated oil (the balls should float in it) until the arancini turns golden.
